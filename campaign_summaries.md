# Ice Age Campaigns - Detailed Summaries

## Campaign 1: "The Forbidden North" 
**Timeline:** During/immediately after Prince Konrad's campaign  
**Main Character:** Frostwhisper - A descendant of ancient ice mages with dormant magical abilities but strong premonition powers

### Background
The northernmost orc tribes have ancient taboos about venturing beyond the Frozen Wastes, believing the ice gods will punish transgressors. When Graksul, a minor orc warrior, faces execution for cowardice in battle, he escapes north across the forbidden ice bridge that forms only during the Week of Deep Freeze.

### Plot Summary
**Scenarios 1-3: The Discovery**
- Graksul discovers the first ice settlements during their most vulnerable time (summer thaw)
- Frostwhisper's premonitions warn of danger, but he's dismissed as a dreamer
- The ice folk have lived in peace for generations, their warrior traditions forgotten

**Scenarios 4-6: The First Strike**
- Graksul returns to challenge and defeat his weakened chieftain
- Leads a scouting party back across the melting ice using crude rafts
- First ice village falls easily - inhabitants flee rather than fight

**Scenarios 7-9: The Invasion Begins**
- Orcs ally with Naga to cross the northern seas
- Multiple coastal settlements are overrun
- Frostwhisper escapes each attack just in time, his reputation as a prophet growing

**Scenarios 10-12: Awakening the Dark**
- A powerful orc shaman named Grimjaw makes contact with ancient evil
- The Frozen Tombs crack open, releasing malevolent spirits
- Frostwhisper must choose between fleeing further inland or making a stand

**Final Scenarios 13-15: The Price of Awakening**
- Ancient dark powers, long imprisoned in ice, begin to stir
- Both orcs and ice folk realize they've unleashed something beyond their control
- Campaign ends with Frostwhisper barely escaping as darkness spreads across the land

## Campaign 2: "The Ice Bleeds Black"
**Timeline:** 5-10 years after Campaign 1  
**Main Character:** Various survivors, primarily Shadowmaw (awakened dark entity) and Grimjaw (the orc shaman who caused this)

### Background
The dark entities awakened from their icy prisons are not mere undead - they are fragments of primordial winter gods, bound long ago by the first ice mages. Their hatred has festered for millennia, and now they seek to turn the entire north into a realm of eternal darkness and ice.

### Plot Summary
**Scenarios 1-4: The Darkness Spreads**
- Play as the dark entities systematically destroying both orc invaders and ice folk
- Ancient curses corrupt the very ice and snow
- Both factions must make desperate alliances just to survive

**Scenarios 5-8: The Orc Betrayal**
- Grimjaw realizes his mistake and tries to banish what he summoned
- Dark forces turn on their orc "allies" with particular vengeance
- Some orc survivors flee south while others are corrupted into shadow-servants

**Scenarios 9-12: The Last Refuges**
- Remaining ice folk retreat to their most ancient strongholds
- Sacred ice temples become battlegrounds between light and darkness
- Frostwhisper, now an elder, makes the ultimate sacrifice to save a group of refugees

**Final Scenarios 13-16: The Bitter Exodus**
- Mass evacuation of survivors begins the long journey south
- Player controls rear-guard actions protecting civilians
- Campaign ends with a handful of survivors reaching the edge of civilized lands
- Final scene: A young ice warrior named Crystalwind reaches Wesnoth's borders

## Campaign 3: "The Long Road South"
**Timeline:** 15-20 years after Campaign 2  
**Main Character:** Crystalwind - Son of a Campaign 1 survivor, raised as a refugee, now a young adult

### Background
The ice folk refugees have spent years wandering the hostile borderlands between the Frozen North and the civilized kingdoms. They've learned to be survivors and warriors, but at the cost of much of their ancient culture. Crystalwind represents a new generation - shaped by loss but determined to reclaim their heritage.

### Plot Summary
**Scenarios 1-3: The Wandering Years**
- Ice folk refugees have become nomadic, moving between temporary camps
- Must negotiate with human border settlements, some friendly, others hostile
- Crystalwind comes of age and takes leadership of his clan

**Scenarios 4-6: Unlikely Alliances**
- Form temporary alliance with dwarf prospectors fleeing orc raids
- Discover that some northern orc tribes are also fleeing the spreading darkness
- Complex three-way dynamics between ice folk, dwarfs, and "refugee orcs"

**Scenarios 7-10: The Mercenary Period**
- Ice folk become sellswords to survive, fighting in human border conflicts
- Crystalwind's tactical genius becomes apparent
- Growing reputation leads to increasingly important contracts

**Scenarios 11-13: The Wesnothian Contact**
- First diplomatic contact with Wesnoth proper
- Must prove their worth by defending a Wesnothian outpost
- Internal conflict: some ice folk want to settle permanently, others want to keep moving south

**Final Scenarios 14-16: Earning Sanctuary**
- Major battle where ice folk prove instrumental in defending Wesnoth's northern border
- Formal recognition and offer of settlement within Wesnoth's protection
- Campaign ends with Crystalwind accepting the offer while secretly planning to eventually return north

## Campaign 4: "The Ice Crown Wars"
**Timeline:** 50+ years after Campaign 3  
**Main Character:** Glacius Stormheart - Crystalwind's grandson, a master of recovered ice magic

### Background
The ice folk have integrated into Wesnothian society but never forgot their homeland. New intelligence suggests the dark powers in the north are weakening, and some areas are becoming habitable again. However, multiple factions now have interests in the resource-rich northern territories. Glacius must navigate complex politics and warfare to reclaim his ancestral lands.

### Plot Summary
**Scenarios 1-5: The Calling North**
- Reports arrive that the northern darkness is retreating
- Glacius convinces the Wesnothian council to support a reclamation expedition
- Multiple factions (dwarfs, humans, orcs, even some elves) launch competing expeditions

**Scenarios 6-10: The Race for Territory**
- Complex multi-faction warfare as each group tries to claim the best lands
- Glacius must balance cooperation and competition
- Discovery that some dark entities still linger in the deepest north

**Scenarios 11-15: The Alliance Web**
- Shifting alliances as external threats (remaining dark entities, hostile orc clans from elsewhere) emerge
- Glacius's diplomatic skills become as important as his military prowess
- Some former enemies become essential allies

**Scenarios 16-20: The Throne of Winter**
- Discovery of the ancient ice folk capital, frozen in time but intact
- Final battle against the last powerful dark entity in the heart of the old kingdom
- Multiple faction armies must coordinate to defeat a threat that could spread south again

**Final Scenarios 21-25: The New Ice Kingdoms**
- Establishment of the new northern settlements
- Glacius must choose between personal power and the good of his people
- Complex ending where success means different things to different factions
- Sets up potential for future conflicts and/or cooperation in the established northern kingdoms

## Campaign Connections and Themes

### Recurring Themes
- **Legacy of Loss:** Each generation carries the trauma of the previous
- **Cultural Evolution:** How a people change when driven from their homeland
- **The Price of Power:** Dark magic/entities always exact a cost
- **Redemption:** Even ancient enemies can find common ground against greater threats

### Character Bloodline
- **Frostwhisper** (Campaign 1): The prophet who saw disaster coming
- **Crystalwind** (Campaign 3): The refugee who found strength in exile  
- **Glacius Stormheart** (Campaign 4): The returner who reclaimed the homeland

### Faction Development Arc
- **Campaign 1:** Peaceful, magically-gifted but militarily weak
- **Campaign 2:** Survivors learning harsh lessons about the world
- **Campaign 3:** Hardened refugees who've become formidable warriors
- **Campaign 4:** A mature people balancing ancient wisdom with hard-won experience

### Political Complexity
Each campaign increases in political sophistication:
- Campaign 1: Simple good vs evil
- Campaign 2: Survival horror with no clear heroes
- Campaign 3: Refugee story with moral grey areas  
- Campaign 4: Complex geopolitical strategy requiring diplomatic finesse
